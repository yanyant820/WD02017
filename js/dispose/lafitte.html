Lafitte

When he purchased the chateau in 1935, Claire Dillen restored it to its former glory and to the elite circle of the most legendary wines in the world. This extraordinary, bold, courageous vision is now continued by the fourth generation of the family, represented by Prince Luxemberg, Chairman since 2008. Located in the town of Pessac, just a few kilometres from Bordeaux, the chateau – the first of the three estates acquired by the Dillon family – is the oldest winegrowing property in the region.

The chateau

The chateau's microclimate is in the heart of a 20 km strip of land along both sides of the Garonne Valley where all of Bordeaux's sweet and semi-sweet white wine appellations (Sainte Croix du Mont, Loupiac, Cérons, Cadillac, and Barsac) are located.

The Yard

The Chateau has 123 hectares of vines, of which only a hundred produce grapes in a given vintage. Two to three hectares of vines that are too old are uprooted annually, and the soil left fallow for a year. Furthermore, it takes at least 5 years before new vines produce grapes that are up to the strict standards. Twelve hectares of vines are thus non-productive every year. The yard is planted with 2 grape varieties: Sémillon (70%), which produces a rich wine with body and structure, and Sauvignon Blanc (30%), an early-ripening but less reliable producer, which contributes aromas and finesse. The vines are tended with the greatest of care in that our crew perpetuate a long tradition of painstaking procedures.
